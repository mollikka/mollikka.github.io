About me

I am Lauri Tervonen, a.k.a. Möllikkä [møl:ik:æ]. I often write my alias as Mollikka as an act of kindness towards all the nice people and computers outside Finland.

I try to be interested in everything, but I do like some things more than others. Some of my interests include plane tilings, fractals, and algorithmic art. I'm also into game design, poetry, and nature photography.

The main reason why I'm studying computer science (and loving it) is that the systematic mindset that comes with programming makes it much easier to answer the type of questions I like to ask. For example, if I didn't understand algorithms, it would have taken me days to manually compute that there are 570 different shapes that can be combined from two Tetris blocks.

Academic

I'm a student in the Computer Science and Engineering programme in Aalto University.

Bachelor's thesis: Strategies in the game Cordial Minuet

Aalto University, Computer Science

I analyzed Cordial Minuet as a zero-sum game and looked for strategies that maximize the winnings in a simplified version of the game. I also found that the choice of game board might have a some effect on expected payoff of the players, which brings an element of luck to the official implementation.

My advisor on the thesis was Louis Theran.
